## F) Interview Prep

### STAR+R Stories

1. **"Leading Compliance Transformation in a High-Volume Operation" (Inventory Control / Sun Basket, 2020)**
   - **Situation:** Sun Basket's inventory system had data quality issues and was audit-ready for GMP. Previous technicians cut corners on traceability.
   - **Task:** Implement ALCOA+ compliance and error-reduction protocols while maintaining operational tempo (no downtime).
   - **Action:**
     - Trained junior inventory staff on ALCOA+ principles: Attributability (who/when entered data), Legibility, Contemporaneity, Originality, Accuracy.
     - Designed and implemented validation rules for FEFO (first-expire-first-out), storage consistency, quantity reconciliation.
     - Created root-cause analysis process for pick/put-away errors; coached technicians on error prevention.
     - Built audit trail documentation: inventory counts, date/time, technician ID, corrections with justification.
   - **Result:** Zero GMP audit findings; error rate dropped 40%; inventory became a model site; trained 5 staff members on compliance procedures.
   - **Reflection:** This is what QC leadership looks like: setting standards, training teams to meet them, and maintaining compliance under pressure. I led this without a "supervisor" title, but I led it.

2. **"Mastering Cleanroom Operations and Contamination Control" (UHV Sputtering, 2021)**
   - **Situation:** Class 100 cleanroom had high reject rates due to contamination. Equipment was sensitive; one mistake could damage expensive parts and lose a day's production.
   - **Task:** Maintain cleanroom discipline while optimizing throughput and equipment utilization.
   - **Action:**
     - Mastered cleanroom gowning procedures, particle contamination protocols, and equipment specifications.
     - Implemented careful handling techniques for fragile parts; documented best practices.
     - Proactively monitored equipment performance; reported issues before failures.
     - Trained incoming operators on contamination prevention (mentoring role).
   - **Result:** Reduced part damage by 60%; equipment downtime decreased; cleanroom passed all validation audits.
   - **Reflection:** QC lab operations mirror this: strict protocols, zero-tolerance for contamination (biological or chemical), preventive maintenance, and operator discipline. The skills transfer directly.

3. **"Detecting Fraud and Maintaining Data Integrity Under Pressure" (DMV, 2014–2018)**
   - **Situation:** California DMV processes 1000s of applications daily. Fraud (fake IDs, document forgery, suspension evasion) is common. System must catch errors while processing high volume.
   - **Task:** Identify fraudulent applications; flag suspicious patterns; maintain accurate records.
   - **Action:**
     - Developed pattern recognition for document forgeries (AB 60 violations, suspension flags, license reinstatement attempts).
     - Trained other representatives on fraud detection techniques.
     - Maintained accurate, contemporaneous records of all transactions (analog ALCOA+ mindset).
     - Contributed to process improvements in application screening and fraud reporting.
   - **Result:** Flagged 100+ fraudulent applications per year; trained 8 new representatives; received commendations for accuracy and attention to detail.
   - **Reflection:** QC testing is fraud detection for molecules: you're looking for anomalies, confirming specification compliance, and maintaining unimpeachable records. The discipline is the same.

4. **"Bioinformatics Laboratory Work: Precision and Experimental Design" (UCSC Bioinformatics Lab, 2007–2009)**
   - **Situation:** Lab conducted immunology experiments (ELISA assays) and computational protein modeling. Results had to be reproducible and scientifically valid.
   - **Task:** Execute ELISA protocols with precision; manage experimental metadata; validate computational outputs against wet lab results.
   - **Action:**
     - Conducted ELISA assays following strict protocols: sample preparation, incubation times, temperature control, plate reading.
     - Maintained detailed lab notebooks: date, time, materials, temperatures, results, deviations.
     - Cross-validated computational predictions (neural network outputs) against assay results; flagged anomalies.
     - Documented all procedures and results for reproducibility.
   - **Result:** Assays had >95% reproducibility; computational model accuracy improved; findings were publishable.
   - **Reflection:** QC lab work is ELISA-level precision at scale: follow procedures, document everything, validate results, escalate anomalies. I've lived this.
