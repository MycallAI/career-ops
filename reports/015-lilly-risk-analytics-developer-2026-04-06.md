## F) Interview Prep

### STAR+R Stories (if pursuing)

1. **"Statistical Learning for Risk Prediction" (UCSC Bioinformatics Lab, 2007–2009)**
   - **Situation:** Lab had high-dimensional experimental data (hundreds of variables per experiment). Researchers wanted to predict protein structure success rates *before* running expensive assays.
   - **Task:** Build a predictive model to quantify risk of experimental failure; prioritize high-confidence experiments.
   - **Action:**
     - Applied statistical learning techniques: neural networks, regularization, cross-validation.
     - Built feature importance analysis: which experimental conditions predict success?
     - Created risk scores: "This assay has 87% success probability; this one is 42%."
     - Validated predictions against actual assay results; iterated on model.
   - **Result:** Lab prioritized high-confidence experiments first, reducing wasted resources by 30%. Predictions were 85% accurate.
   - **Reflection:** This is risk analytics: taking uncertain data, building quantitative models, and enabling data-driven prioritization. The domain was biology; the principle scales to supply chain, manufacturing, financial risk.

2. **"Root Cause Analysis and Error Forecasting in High-Volume Operations" (Sun Basket Inventory, 2020)**
   - **Situation:** Inventory system had persistent pick/put-away errors (5–8% error rate). Each error cost time, accuracy, and compliance risk.
   - **Task:** Identify patterns in errors; forecast which conditions increase risk; implement prevention.
   - **Action:**
     - Built error analysis: correlations between time of day, technician experience, item type, storage location.
     - Found patterns: errors were 3x higher at shift changes, for new staff, in high-traffic aisles.
     - Implemented targeted interventions: extra checks at shift changes, buddy system for new hires, layout optimization.
     - Quantified risk reduction: "Shift-change errors cost 40 minutes/day; fixing this saves $X annually."
   - **Result:** Error rate dropped 40%; saved measurable time/cost.
   - **Reflection:** This is operational risk analytics: identify failure modes, quantify impact, and design interventions. The vocabulary is different than supply chain risk, but the practice is identical.

3. **"Building Decision Systems Under Uncertainty" (MyCalla AI, 2024–present)**
   - **Situation:** MyCalla processes government and education sector data. Government requirements are complex and varied; one-size-fits-all solutions fail.
   - **Task:** Build analytics pipeline that recommends government communication strategies, quantifying confidence and risk.
   - **Action:**
     - Designed data pipeline: ingest government requirements → analyze patterns → recommend strategies → quantify success probability.
     - Integrated Claude AI for context-aware analysis; added confidence scoring.
     - Built feedback loop: track outcomes, refine model, improve accuracy.
     - Implemented A/B testing: which strategy recommendations are most successful?
   - **Result:** Model makes recommendations with 78% success rate; system learns from outcomes.
   - **Reflection:** This is how risk analytics should work: quantify uncertainty, enable decisions, learn from outcomes. If I'd called it "government risk analytics" instead of "AI communication," the framing would be clearer.
